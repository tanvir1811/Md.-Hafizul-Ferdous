Specific Relief Act


Certainly! The Specific Relief Act, 1877 in Bangladesh defines and amends the law related to specific relief obtainable in civil suits. Here are some key points:
1. Types of Specific Relief:
- Recovery of Property:
-Immovable Property: Allows for the recovery of specific immovable property.
-Moveable Property: Enables the recovery of specific moveable property.
- Specific Performance of Contracts:
- Enforces specific performance of valid contracts.
- Includes cases where part of the contract remains unperformed.
- Court discretion applies.
- Rectification of Instruments:
- Rectifies instruments (e.g., contracts) when necessary.
2. Discretion of the Court:
- The court may declare a person's entitlement to legal character or property rights.
- No declaration if further relief beyond title is available.
3. How Specific Relief Is Given:
- By taking possession of property.
- By ordering an obligated act.
- By preventing prohibited actions.
- By determining rights without compensation.
- By appointing a receiver.
